Saturn's Moons

Overview

Saturn has at least 83 known moons, with Titan being the largest and the second-largest moon in the Solar System. Many of Saturn's moons are icy worlds, and some, like Enceladus, have subsurface oceans and geysers that shoot water into space.

Key Facts

  • Largest moon: Titan (larger than Mercury)
  • Enceladus has active geysers and a subsurface ocean
  • Many moons are named after Titans from Greek mythology
  • Some moons are shepherds for Saturn's rings
  • Moons range in size from larger than Mercury to less than a kilometer

Did You Know?

  • Titan has a thick atmosphere and lakes of liquid methane and ethane.
  • Enceladus is one of the most reflective bodies in the Solar System.
  • Some of Saturn's moons may be captured asteroids or comets.
