Space Facts

Jupiter

Overview

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest in the Solar System. It is a gas giant with a mass more than two and a half times that of all the other planets combined. Jupiter is known for its Great Red Spot, a giant storm larger than Earth, and its many moons.

Key Facts

  • Diameter: 139,820 km
  • Distance from Sun: ~778.5 million km (5.2 AU)
  • Orbital period: 11.86 Earth years
  • Atmosphere: Mostly hydrogen and helium
  • Moons: At least 79, including Ganymede (largest in the Solar System)
  • Famous for its Great Red Spot

Did You Know?

  • Jupiter has the strongest magnetic field of any planet in the Solar System.
  • Its day is the shortest of all planets: about 10 hours.
  • Jupiter's moon Ganymede is larger than Mercury.
