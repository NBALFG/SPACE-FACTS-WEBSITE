Space Facts

Saturn

Overview

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest in the Solar System. It is best known for its spectacular ring system, which is made up of ice and rock particles. Saturn is a gas giant with a low density and dozens of moons, including Titan, which is larger than Mercury.

Key Facts

  • Diameter: 116,460 km
  • Distance from Sun: ~1.43 billion km (9.58 AU)
  • Orbital period: 29.45 Earth years
  • Atmosphere: Mostly hydrogen and helium
  • Moons: At least 83, including Titan (second-largest in the Solar System)
  • Famous for its extensive ring system

Did You Know?

  • Saturn is the least dense planet; it would float in water if a large enough body of water existed.
  • Its rings are made mostly of water ice, with some dust and rock.
  • Saturn's moon Enceladus has geysers that shoot water into space.
