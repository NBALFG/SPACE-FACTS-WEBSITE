Space Facts

Exoplanets

Overview

Exoplanets are planets that orbit stars outside our Solar System. Thousands have been discovered, with a wide variety of sizes, compositions, and orbits. Some exoplanets may be similar to Earth and could potentially support life.

Key Facts

  • First confirmed exoplanet discovered in 1992
  • Over 5,000 exoplanets confirmed as of 2023
  • Methods of detection: transit, radial velocity, direct imaging
  • Some exoplanets are in the "habitable zone" of their stars
  • Exoplanets can be gas giants, rocky, or ocean worlds

Did You Know?

  • Kepler-186f was the first Earth-size exoplanet found in the habitable zone of its star.
  • Some exoplanets orbit two stars (circumbinary planets).
  • NASA's Kepler and TESS missions have discovered thousands of exoplanets.
