Pluto

Overview

Pluto is a dwarf planet in the Kuiper Belt, a region of icy bodies beyond Neptune. Once considered the ninth planet, Pluto was reclassified as a dwarf planet in 2006. It has a thin atmosphere and five known moons, the largest of which is Charon.

Key Facts

  • Diameter: 2,377 km
  • Distance from Sun: ~5.9 billion km (39.5 AU)
  • Orbital period: 248 Earth years
  • Atmosphere: Nitrogen, methane, carbon monoxide (thin and variable)
  • Moons: 5 (Charon, Styx, Nix, Kerberos, Hydra)
  • Surface temperature: -229Â°C

Did You Know?

  • Pluto's orbit is highly elliptical and sometimes brings it closer to the Sun than Neptune.
  • Charon is so large relative to Pluto that they are sometimes considered a double dwarf planet system.
  • Pluto was discovered in 1930 by Clyde Tombaugh.
