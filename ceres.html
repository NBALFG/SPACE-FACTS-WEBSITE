Space Facts

Ceres

Overview

Ceres is the largest object in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter and is classified as a dwarf planet. It is the only dwarf planet located in the inner Solar System and was the first to be visited by a spacecraft (Dawn).

Key Facts

  • Diameter: 940 km
  • Distance from Sun: ~413.7 million km (2.77 AU)
  • Orbital period: 4.6 Earth years
  • Surface temperature: -106°C to -38°C
  • May have a subsurface ocean of liquid water
  • Discovered in 1801 by Giuseppe Piazzi

Did You Know?

  • Ceres has bright spots that are deposits of sodium carbonate, likely from briny water.
  • It makes up about a third of the mass of the entire asteroid belt.
  • Ceres is named after the Roman goddess of agriculture.
