Space Facts

Asteroids

Overview

Asteroids are small, rocky objects that orbit the Sun, mostly found in the Asteroid Belt between Mars and Jupiter. They are remnants from the early Solar System and vary greatly in size and shape.

Key Facts

  • Largest: Ceres, Vesta, Pallas, Hygiea
  • Most are irregularly shaped
  • Some have moons or are binary systems
  • Can be classified as C-type, S-type, or M-type
  • Some asteroids cross Earth's orbit (Near-Earth Objects)

Did You Know?

  • Asteroids are sometimes called minor planets or planetoids.
  • NASA's OSIRIS-REx mission collected a sample from asteroid Bennu.
  • Some meteorites found on Earth are fragments of asteroids.
