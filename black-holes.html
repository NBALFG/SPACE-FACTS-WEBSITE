Black Holes

Overview

Black holes are regions of spacetime where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape. They are formed from the remnants of massive stars after supernova explosions or by the merging of smaller black holes. The boundary around a black hole is called the event horizon.

Key Facts

  • Types: Stellar, Intermediate, Supermassive, Primordial
  • Event horizon: The point of no return
  • Supermassive black holes exist at the centers of most galaxies
  • First image of a black hole (M87*) was captured in 2019

Did You Know?

  • Time slows down near a black hole due to extreme gravity (gravitational time dilation).
  • Black holes can "evaporate" over time via Hawking radiation.
  • The nearest known black hole to Earth is about 1,500 light-years away.
