Nebulas

Overview

Nebulas are vast clouds of gas and dust in space, often serving as the birthplaces of stars. They come in several types, including emission, reflection, and dark nebulas. Some of the most famous nebulas are visible to the naked eye from Earth.

Key Facts

  • Types: Emission, reflection, dark, planetary
  • Famous examples: Orion Nebula, Eagle Nebula, Crab Nebula
  • Can span light-years across
  • Sites of star formation
  • Composed mainly of hydrogen, helium, and dust

Did You Know?

  • The Orion Nebula is about 1,344 light-years from Earth.
  • Planetary nebulas are formed from the outer layers of dying stars.
  • The colors in nebula images often represent different elements or are enhanced for scientific study.
