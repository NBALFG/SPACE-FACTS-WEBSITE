Jupiter's Moons

Overview

Jupiter has at least 79 known moons, the largest number of any planet in the Solar System. The four largest, known as the Galilean moons (Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto), were discovered by Galileo Galilei in 1610 and are among the most fascinating objects in the Solar System.

Key Facts

  • Galilean moons: Io, Europa, Ganymede, Callisto
  • Ganymede is the largest moon in the Solar System
  • Io is the most volcanically active body in the Solar System
  • Europa may have a subsurface ocean
  • Many smaller moons are irregularly shaped and captured asteroids

Did You Know?

  • Ganymede is larger than the planet Mercury.
  • Europa is a prime target in the search for extraterrestrial life.
  • Jupiter's strong gravity helps protect the inner Solar System from some comets and asteroids.
