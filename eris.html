Eris

Overview

Eris is one of the largest known dwarf planets in the Solar System and is located in the scattered disc, a distant region beyond the Kuiper Belt. Its discovery in 2005 was a major factor in the redefinition of planets and the reclassification of Pluto as a dwarf planet.

Key Facts

  • Diameter: 2,326 km
  • Distance from Sun: ~10.1 billion km (67.7 AU)
  • Orbital period: 558 Earth years
  • Atmosphere: Possibly thin, temporary (mainly nitrogen and methane)
  • Moon: 1 (Dysnomia)

Did You Know?

  • Eris is slightly smaller than Pluto but more massive.
  • Its orbit is highly elliptical and inclined to the plane of the Solar System.
  • Eris was temporarily considered the "tenth planet" after its discovery.
