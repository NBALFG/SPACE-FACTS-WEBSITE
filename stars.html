Space Facts

Stars

Overview

Stars are massive, luminous spheres of plasma held together by gravity. They are the fundamental building blocks of galaxies and are born in nebulae. Stars vary in size, mass, temperature, and color, and their life cycles range from millions to billions of years.

Key Facts

  • The closest star to Earth is the Sun
  • Stars are classified by spectral type (O, B, A, F, G, K, M)
  • Most stars are part of binary or multiple star systems
  • Stars end their lives as white dwarfs, neutron stars, or black holes
  • There are billions of stars in our galaxy alone

Did You Know?

  • Some stars are more than 100 times more massive than the Sun.
  • Red dwarfs are the most common type of star in the universe.
  • Supernovae are powerful explosions marking the end of a massive star's life.
