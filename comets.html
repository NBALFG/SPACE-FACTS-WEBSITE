Space Facts

Comets

Overview

Comets are icy bodies that release gas and dust, forming a glowing coma and tail when near the Sun. They originate from the Kuiper Belt and Oort Cloud and have highly elliptical orbits.

Key Facts

  • Composed of ice, dust, and rocky material
  • Develop a coma and tail when close to the Sun
  • Famous comets: Halley's Comet, Comet Hale-Bopp, Comet 67P
  • Orbits can take thousands or even millions of years
  • Source of meteor showers on Earth

Did You Know?

  • Comet tails always point away from the Sun due to the solar wind.
  • Halley's Comet is visible from Earth every 76 years.
  • NASA's Deep Impact and ESA's Rosetta missions studied comets up close.
