Milky Way Galaxy

Overview

The Milky Way is a barred spiral galaxy that contains our Solar System. It is estimated to have over 100 billion stars and is about 100,000 light-years in diameter. Our solar system is located in the Orion Arm, about 27,000 light-years from the galactic center.

Key Facts

  • Type: Barred spiral galaxy
  • Diameter: ~100,000 light-years
  • Number of stars: 100–400 billion
  • Distance to center: ~27,000 light-years
  • Contains a supermassive black hole at its center (Sagittarius A*)

Did You Know?

  • The Milky Way and Andromeda galaxies are on a collision course and will merge in about 4.5 billion years.
  • Most of the mass of the Milky Way is in the form of dark matter.
  • The name "Milky Way" comes from its appearance as a dim, milky band of light in the night sky.
