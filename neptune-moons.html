Neptune's Moons

Overview

Neptune has at least 14 known moons, with Triton being the largest and most geologically active. Triton has geysers that spout nitrogen gas and is thought to be a captured Kuiper Belt object. The other moons are much smaller and irregularly shaped.

Key Facts

  • Largest moon: Triton (retrograde orbit)
  • Triton has geysers and a thin nitrogen atmosphere
  • Other moons are much smaller and irregularly shaped
  • Discovered in 1846 (Triton) and later
  • Triton is geologically active and may have a subsurface ocean

Did You Know?

  • Triton is the only large moon in the Solar System with a retrograde orbit.
  • Triton is slowly spiraling inward and will eventually be torn apart by Neptune's gravity.
  • Voyager 2 is the only spacecraft to have visited Neptune and its moons.
