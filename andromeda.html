Andromeda Galaxy

Overview

The Andromeda Galaxy (M31) is the closest spiral galaxy to the Milky Way and is on a collision course with our galaxy. It is about 2.5 million light-years from Earth and is the largest galaxy in the Local Group, which also includes the Milky Way, Triangulum Galaxy, and about 54 other smaller galaxies.

Key Facts

  • Type: Spiral galaxy (SA(s)b)
  • Distance from Earth: ~2.54 million light-years
  • Diameter: ~220,000 light-years
  • Number of stars: ~1 trillion
  • Will merge with the Milky Way in about 4.5 billion years

Did You Know?

  • The Andromeda Galaxy is visible to the naked eye from Earth under dark skies.
  • It is also known as Messier 31, M31, or NGC 224.
  • Andromeda and the Milky Way are moving toward each other at about 110 km/s.
