Solar Eclipse

Overview

A solar eclipse occurs when the Moon passes between the Earth and the Sun, temporarily blocking the Sun's light. There are several types of solar eclipses: total, partial, and annular. Eclipses can only occur during a new moon.

Key Facts

  • Types: Total, Partial, Annular, Hybrid
  • Occurs only during a new moon
  • Totality can last up to 7.5 minutes
  • The path of totality is usually only about 100 miles wide
  • Solar eclipses are rare at any given location

Did You Know?

  • Looking directly at a solar eclipse without protection can cause permanent eye damage.
  • Solar eclipses helped prove Einstein's theory of general relativity in 1919.
  • There are at least two solar eclipses somewhere on Earth every year.
