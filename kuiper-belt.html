Kuiper Belt

Overview

The Kuiper Belt is a vast region beyond Neptune filled with icy bodies, dwarf planets, and comets. It is similar to the Asteroid Belt but much larger and is the source of many short-period comets.

Key Facts

  • Distance from Sun: ~30 to 55 AU
  • Contains Pluto, Haumea, Makemake, and other dwarf planets
  • Estimated to contain hundreds of thousands of icy bodies
  • Source of short-period comets
  • Discovered in 1992

Did You Know?

  • The Kuiper Belt is named after astronomer Gerard Kuiper.
  • The New Horizons spacecraft flew through the Kuiper Belt after visiting Pluto.
  • The Oort Cloud is a more distant, spherical shell of icy bodies beyond the Kuiper Belt.
