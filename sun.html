Space Facts

The Sun

Overview

The Sun is a G-type main-sequence star (G2V) at the center of our solar system. It is by far the largest object in the solar system, containing more than 99.8% of its total mass. The Sun's energy supports almost all life on Earth via photosynthesis and drives Earth's climate and weather.

Key Facts

  • Diameter: 1,391,000 km
  • Mass: 1.989 × 1030 kg
  • Surface temperature: ~5,500°C
  • Core temperature: ~15 million °C
  • Distance from Earth: ~149.6 million km (1 AU)
  • Age: ~4.6 billion years

Did You Know?

  • Light from the Sun takes about 8 minutes and 20 seconds to reach Earth.
  • The Sun is about halfway through its main-sequence life.
  • Solar flares and coronal mass ejections can affect Earth's magnetic field and technology.
