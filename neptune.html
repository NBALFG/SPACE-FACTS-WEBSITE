Space Facts

Neptune

Overview

Neptune is the eighth and farthest known planet from the Sun in the Solar System. It is an ice giant, similar to Uranus, and is known for its deep blue color and supersonic winds. Neptune has a faint ring system and 14 known moons, including Triton.

Key Facts

  • Diameter: 49,244 km
  • Distance from Sun: ~4.5 billion km (30.1 AU)
  • Orbital period: 164.8 Earth years
  • Atmosphere: Hydrogen, helium, methane
  • Moons: 14, including Triton
  • Strongest winds in the Solar System (up to 2,100 km/h)

Did You Know?

  • Neptune was discovered in 1846 based on mathematical predictions.
  • Triton, Neptune's largest moon, orbits in the opposite direction to Neptune's rotation (retrograde orbit).
  • Neptune appears blue due to methane in its atmosphere absorbing red light.
