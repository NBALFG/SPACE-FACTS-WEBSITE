Asteroid Belt

Overview

The Asteroid Belt is a region of space between the orbits of Mars and Jupiter where millions of rocky bodies called asteroids orbit the Sun. It contains the dwarf planet Ceres and is thought to be remnants from the early Solar System that never formed into a planet.

Key Facts

  • Main objects: Ceres, Vesta, Pallas, Hygiea
  • Distance from Sun: ~2.2 to 3.2 AU
  • Contains millions of asteroids
  • Total mass is less than Earth's Moon
  • Asteroids are irregularly shaped and vary in size

Did You Know?

  • Some asteroids have their own moons.
  • Spacecraft have visited and landed on several asteroids.
  • The Asteroid Belt is not densely packed; spacecraft can easily pass through it.
