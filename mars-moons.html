Mars' Moons

Overview

Mars has two small moons, Phobos and Deimos, which are thought to be captured asteroids from the asteroid belt or Kuiper Belt. Both moons are irregularly shaped and much smaller than Earth's Moon.

Key Facts

  • Phobos: 22.2 km diameter, orbits Mars in 7.7 hours
  • Deimos: 12.4 km diameter, orbits Mars in 30.3 hours
  • Both are irregularly shaped and heavily cratered
  • Phobos is slowly spiraling toward Mars
  • Discovered in 1877 by Asaph Hall

Did You Know?

  • Phobos will eventually crash into Mars or break up into a ring.
  • Deimos is slowly moving away from Mars.
  • Both moons are named after the sons of Ares (Mars) in Greek mythology.
